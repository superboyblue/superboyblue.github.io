.each_slice is a handy little enumerable method that does exactly what it sounds like it
does, it slices, it dices! It makes everything tiny and managable.

Say you give it a number, any number like... 10. And you want to slice it up into parts.
We'll say teams of 3. And then there's a number left all by itself. It sucks to be in your
own little slice sometimes. Unless it's pie. Then those people in teams have to share a
slice amongst the 3 of them and you can have your own little slice to yourself.


And if you want to slice an array? You can do that. Just give it an array of whatever
you want, and it'll slice it into arrays with the number of elements you specified. Pass it
in a block and it'll print out all your slices. Pretty neat, huh? If it isn't evenly
divisible, the remaining elements will once again be put in the final slice.




It works on hashes, too. With a block, the same rules apply. It'll split your hash into
arrays of the number that you wanted, putting the remainders in the final slice. The arrays
themselves contain the key and value pair of the hash element.

There's also this thing where if you don't give it a code block and just a number, it'll
return an enumerator and you need to use the .next method to see your slices but that's not
really too helpful right now so we won't get into it. Until next time!
